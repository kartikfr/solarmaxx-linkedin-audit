Diagnosis

SolarMaxx posts regularly and has 12K people following. That's the good news. The bad news is that 90% of the content is generic festival celebrations with no link to solar energy, no story, and nothing worth sharing. The audience is there. They're just not being given a reason to engage.

01

What's Not Working

Six issues behind the 2.1 score

Critical

Festival Content Is Dominating the Feed

Women's Day got 8 likes. Holi got 6. Republic Day got 6. Each caption is one flat line with no connection to solar energy. The page reads like a national holiday calendar, not a solar company's voice.

Fix

Cap celebrations at 10% of posts. Every festival mention needs to connect back to the solar mission — something only SolarMaxx could say.

Critical

Captions Have No Structure or Purpose

Every caption is one line. No story, no question, no reason to click "see more" or leave a comment. Readers have nothing to react to, so they don't.

Every caption needs this structure
1
Hook: one line that earns the click to "see more"
2
Context: what's happening and why it matters
3
Insight: what the reader takes away from this
4
CTA: one clear ask — comment, DM, share, follow
High Priority

No Consistent Visual Brand

Illustrated stock graphics, inconsistent colors, a logo that's small and easy to miss. A viewer scrolling past cannot identify a SolarMaxx post in under a second — which means they won't stop for it.

Fix

Lock in a design system: SolarMaxx blue and gold, one font pair, logo top-right on every post. Five templates, used consistently, build recognition fast.

Critical

Not a Single Video on the Timeline

Video is LinkedIn's best-performing format by a wide margin — five times the engagement of static posts. SolarMaxx has zero. This is the largest single missed opportunity on the page right now.

Fix

Start with one CEO video per week. 60 seconds. A direct take on something happening in Indian solar. xto100x handles the script and production brief.

High Priority

Followers Don't Know What SolarMaxx Does

No posts on solar policy, PM-KUSUM, net metering, DISCOM ratings, installation guides, or project results. After scrolling the feed, a potential customer has no idea what SolarMaxx offers or why they should trust them.

Fix

Forty percent of posts should be education or industry commentary. Own the solar conversation in your geography — don't leave that to competitors.

Critical

Engagement Is Far Below the Benchmark

Accounts with 10K to 15K followers typically see 30 to 80 likes per post with decent content. SolarMaxx is averaging 6 to 8. The gap isn't the audience size — it's the content. Generic posts with no CTAs give people no reason to interact.

Fix

Every post needs a hook at the start and a clear CTA at the end. Content that's genuinely useful or interesting earns engagement — celebration graphics don't.
